Omnitrans is the public transit agency serving the San Bernardino Valley in California.
Established in 1976 through a joint powers agreement, it provides safe,
reliable, affordable, and environmentally friendly transportation.
Omnitrans operates local and express bus routes, sbX bus rapid transit service,
and Access, a paratransit service for individuals with disabilities.
The agency covers a 480-square mile service area, including 15 cities and parts of unincorporated San Bernardino County.
Omnitrans carries approximately 6 million passengers annually, connecting major destinations such as transportation centers,
medical facilities, educational institutions, shopping malls,
business parks, and community centers.
Omnitrans' fleet includes clean fuel transit coaches and minibuses powered by gasoline, CNG, and electric engines.
The agency is committed to future growth and sustainability,
with projects like the West Valley Connector Corridor aimed at addressing increasing traffic congestion and population growth.
